RfK Help Desk

1. {% trans %}Installing the streaming software{% endtrans %}:

In order to participate you need a piece of software which transmits your audio signal (music, speech, whatever) to our streaming server. We suggest that you use one of the following clients, but you can also look for alternatives here or by browsing the Internet yourself.

Our backend supports the icecast2 streaming protocol, so every client which supports icecast2 should be working just fine with RfK.

Tip: We highly recommend edcast when using Windows and idjc when using Linux. Don’t use OSX if you don’t have to. We’re serious.

Windows:

  • edcast (foobar2000 plugin, WinAmp plugin, Standalone)
  • edcast-reborn (WinAmp plugin, Standalone)
  • Mixxx (DJ software with streaming support)

Linux:

  • idjc (Internet DJ Console, experts only)
  • darkice (command-line interface)

Mac OS X:

  • Nicecast (unable to transmit metadata)
  • Ladiocast (unable to transmit metadata)

2. {% trans %}Setting up your streaming software{% endtrans %}:

Connection settings:

After installing the appropriate software you have to configure your client to connect to our streaming server. Here are the basic settings you need to enter in your streaming client. But beware: some settings may be called different depending on your client.

Hostname radio.krautchan.net
Port 8080
Mountpoint /live.ogg
Username your RfK username
Password your RfK password
Tip: Some clients won’t let you specify a separate username; you then have to enter a combination of your username and password in the password field of your client. The Syntax we use is as follows: username|password, where "|" (vertical bar) is the delimiter.

Encoding:

Despite the fact that the mountpoint is called /live.ogg, the server accepts many containers and not just ogg.
Anyhow: we recommend using ogg/vorbis to be on the safe side, since we've encountered some problems with clients using mp3.

Quality:

Depending on your available upstream you can set the bitrate, and hence the quality of your audio signal.
We recommend using at least 192kbps or q4 when using ogg/vorbis.

Examples:

edcast:

Tip: If you have installed the WinAmp or foobar2000 version of edcast don’t forget to activate the plugin first. (DSP Manager)

  1. Open the edcast main window
  2. Click Add Encoder
  3. Double click the newly added encoder
  4. Enter your settings (see above)
  5. Confirm by clicking OK
  6. Press Connect to stream

Nicecast:

Tip: Press ⌘ + 2 to open the server window when Nicecast is running. Reminder: Sending metadata won’t work with this client.
